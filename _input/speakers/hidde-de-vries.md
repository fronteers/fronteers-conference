The first rule of ARIA suggests it is better avoided if there is an HTML equivalent. Yet sometimes, the right use of ARIA can improve your UI better for end users. In this talk, we’ll look at practical examples of effective ARIA usage and how they work in assistive technologies. Find out more about the patterns that have broad support and which ones to avoid. Let’s look at ARIA, the good parts!

---

Hidde de Vries ([@hdv@front-end.social](https://front-end.social/@hdv)) is a web enthusiast, accessibility specialist and [conference speaker](/speaking) from Rotterdam (The Netherlands). He currently works with the [NL Design System](https://nldesignsystem.nl) team and is a participant in the [Open UI Community Group](//open-ui.org/). Previously, he worked for W3C (WAI), Mozilla, the Dutch government and others as a freelancer.
